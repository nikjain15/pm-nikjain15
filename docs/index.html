What it does

An AI teammate that keeps the board true

◑

Senses your work

Reads your public commits and PRs and moves cards through to do → in progress → done on its own. No dragging.

✎

Writes your week

A model-written brief in plain language — what you shipped, where the team stands — posted the moment it happens.

◆

An agent you talk to

“Move the login card to done.” “Plan my week.” Do or ask, in plain English — and it remembers your context across visits.

⇌

Connects the team

Spots when two people are working on the same thing — from public work — and suggests they compare notes.

◇

Momentum, not a scoreboard

Collective momentum you can feel — shipping streaks, help passed on. Never a leaderboard, never a rank.

⛨

Consent by design

Facts are public; a sentence written about you needs your yes. Turn it off, make it ask first, or delete anything it posted — any time.

Use it for your own project

Pulse is open source — make it yours

Pulse runs on Next.js and Firebase and deploys to Vercel. To point it at your own team's project:

  1. Fork the repo: github.com/nikjain15/pulse
  2. Create a Firebase project and drop your web config into the app's env.
  3. Set the public GitHub repo Pulse should read — that's your team's work source.
  4. Add an ANTHROPIC_API_KEY so Pulse can write your week and answer in the agent panel.
  5. Deploy to Vercel. Your team signs in, and the board starts keeping itself current.

The design that matters

It knows who's quiet — and it never tells

Pulse can see everything about a team. What it chooses not to do is the point.

No leaderboard, ever

Kudos are recognition, not a score. Nothing here ranks people by output.

Never punish the quiet

No streaks, no “N days inactive”, no absence counts shown to the team. When in doubt, it says nothing.

Facts vs. narrative

Merged PRs are public record. A model-written sentence about a person needs their opt-in — no exceptions.

Degrade loudly, never fake

An empty week says so. A stale board is never dressed up as live. Seven real people beat sixty invented ones.
